| 1 | Rigor of method / argument | 6 | 4 | The refinement-spectrum method is sound and the separation argument (extensionality + top-world lemma) is clean and kernel-checked. But Theorem 4.3 as printed — "A Boolean valuation $v : S \to \mathrm{Bool}$ is the profile of some world of $\mathrm{Horn}(S, \mathit{cs})$ iff $v$ satisfies every clause" (— §4, thm:horn-boundary) — is attributed to `horn_realizable_iff`, whose actual Lean statement is the global biconditional (every assignment realizable iff every clause is trivial), not the per-valuation boundary. One of the five headline contributions (C4) rests on a statement the kernel did not check; the downstream separation chain (Lemma 5.3, Thm 5.4) is independently sound. |
| 2 | Evidence sufficiency | 6 | 4 | The evidence base is exceptional in kind: "the spectrum, Horn, equivalence, and exclusion developments comprise 63 declarations" (— §1), and the audit re-verified the build, the axiom footprint, and all 42 Appendix cross-references against the artifact. However the evidence for contribution (C4) as stated is absent — no declaration proving the per-valuation Horn boundary exists in any of the four modules — and the §5 proof-length claim ("nine lines") is factually wrong (six tactic lines). A claim without its proof is a hypothesis. |
